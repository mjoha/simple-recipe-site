  • Derp Soup

    Soup · Beginner · 35 minutes · Family notebook

    Introduction

    Derp soup should be bright and comforting at the same time, not thin or sugary.

    Objective

    A balanced bowl with body, acidity, and warmth.

    Ingredients

    Onion, garlic, canned tomatoes, olive oil, stock or water, and salt.

    Preparation

    Slice onion and garlic before heating the pot. Keep extra stock nearby to adjust consistency.

    Execution

    Cook onion gently in olive oil until soft.

    Add garlic briefly.

    Add tomatoes and stock, then simmer until integrated.

    Blend to your preferred texture.

    Season in small steps.

    Reflection

    If flavor feels flat, it usually needs salt first. If still dull, add a little acidity. If sharp, simmer longer.

    Variation

    Finish with cream for a softer profile, or chili flakes for heat.

  • Scrambled Eggs

    Eggs · Gentle but unforgiving · 10 minutes

    Introduction

    Bad scrambled eggs are rubber and regret. Good scrambled eggs feel almost unfairly luxurious for such a short recipe.

    Objective

    A soft, creamy scramble that still tastes cleanly of egg.

    Ingredients

    Eggs, butter, and fine salt.

    Preparation

    Use a small saucepan and a flexible spatula. Keep salt beside the stove. Crack eggs into a bowl and beat until uniform.

    Execution

    Melt butter over gentle heat.

    Add eggs and stir slowly, sweeping the bottom constantly.

    Pull the pan off heat before the eggs look fully done.

    Let residual heat finish the texture.

    Taste, then salt.

    Reflection

    Most misses come from too much heat or too much confidence. If eggs seize, lower heat next time. If they dry out, stop earlier.

    Variation

    Better eggs improve everything.

    A spoon of creme fraiche adds extra silk.

    Chives and black pepper are optional finishing notes.
